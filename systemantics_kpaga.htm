The electronic component soldering robot was one of my projects at Systemantics. ASYSTR 400 is capable of panning a diametric workspace of 400mm with a repeatability of 100μm at the tool tip, and was designed to be controlled using a native android application. The robots were on display at the Systemantics outpost as part of the International Machine Tools Exhibition in 2017.

SYSTEM INTEGRATION

 

 

I developed the control software for executing straight line and also 3D spline (smooth trajectory) paths for executing complicated manoeuvres at uniform speed. Further, I implemented our core (inter-robot) distributed control architecture at both the joint and central controller layers that increased our ability to fabricate and test sub-system technologies as independent plug & play units for any manipulator configuration.

All the software was released on-board ©TI microcontrollers for integration with the actuators, central-controller and application interface modules.

PLANAR PARALLEL MECHANISM

 

ASYSTR 400’s unique planar parallel mechanism provides positioning of the tool tip in the horizontal plane combined with a swivel and lift mechanism that provides vertical motion and rotation for the tool about the vertical axis. Implementing the software for A400’s kinematic model was a task that I accomplished and tested rigorously. One of the interesting sub-tasks was to accommodate hundreds of taught points in the primary memory while also executing the robotic control processes.

 

APPLICATION DEVELOPMENT

 

Working with sales and marketing I went through lean processes of developing several applications that demonstrated not only the capabilities of the system, but also fine-tuned operational parameters of the product itself.

Other applications included:

·         Press tending

·         Palletizing turbine micro-fins  for further machining

·         Gluing operation for 4 wheeler headlights

·         Electrochemical deburring for axles of heavy duty trucks

Express Homing

A motion sensor was embedded into the mechanical body of the robot, to allow for sensing its relative position. This principle was furthered to reduce the initializing sequence of the robot, and thus the homing time to less than half a minute.

Advances

Starting July 2017, the technology developed for ASYSTR 400 has been forked to form a 6 degree of freedom ASYSTR600 manipulator.
